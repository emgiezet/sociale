15 years of PHP. Then I learned Python. Then Go. Now AI. Here's what every pivot taught me.

I didn't plan any of these transitions. Each one happened because the problem I was solving outgrew the tool I was using.

**PHP/Symfony (15 years)**
I built serious production software in PHP. Led open-source contributions to SonataAdminBundle — 18 million installs. PHP taught me that "boring" technology that ships reliably beats "exciting" technology that doesn't. I still believe that.

**Python**
The AI ecosystem lived in Python. I didn't switch because Python was better in some abstract sense. I switched because the libraries I needed didn't exist elsewhere. The lesson: follow the ecosystem that solves your actual problem, not the one that wins Twitter arguments.

**Go**
Concurrency, performance, operational simplicity. Go taught me that constraints produce clarity. A language that forces you to handle errors explicitly changes how you think about failure modes — which matters a lot when you're building systems people depend on.

**AI/RAG**
This one is different. AI is not a language pivot. It's a paradigm shift. You're no longer writing deterministic logic. You're shaping probabilistic systems and then measuring whether they behave well enough to be trusted. That requires a different kind of engineering discipline — and a lot more humility.

Every pivot looked risky from the outside. From the inside, each one was just the next logical step.

**The engineers who stay relevant aren't the ones who picked the right language. They're the ones who kept moving when the problem changed.**
