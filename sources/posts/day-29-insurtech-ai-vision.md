In 2 years, every insurance company will need an AI team. Most aren't building one. Here's the playbook.

I run AI engineering at Insly. I watch InsurTech from the inside. I talk to other engineering leaders in the space. The gap between what's possible and what most insurance companies are actually building is widening, not narrowing.

Here's what the landscape looks like from where I sit — and what I think the next two years demand.

**What's coming:**

By 2027–2028, AI-assisted underwriting, broker copilots, automated claims triage, and intelligent policy search will not be differentiators. They'll be baseline expectations. The companies building now will have production-hardened systems, proprietary evaluation frameworks, and teams that know how to iterate. The companies starting in 2028 will be buying vendor solutions and hoping the compliance story holds.

**The trap most companies are walking into:**

Signing enterprise contracts with AI vendors instead of building internal capability. Vendor solutions are fine for getting started. They're a liability when the vendor changes pricing, deprecates a feature, or can't handle your country-specific regulatory edge cases. Insurance is too domain-specific to outsource the core.

**The playbook — 5 steps:**

1. Hire one or two engineers who understand both AI fundamentals and your domain. Not AI generalists — people who will learn your data.

2. Start with a narrow, high-value problem. Policy search or broker FAQ assistance, not end-to-end automation. Ship something real to real users in under 6 months.

3. Build your evaluation framework before scaling. Define what good looks like. Measure retrieval quality, answer faithfulness, user satisfaction. Make it automated.

4. Treat compliance as architecture, not a review step. GDPR, insurance regulation, and AI Act requirements need to be designed in from day one. Retrofitting compliance is 3x the work.

5. Iterate in production, not in staging. The feedback loop from real users in a regulated environment is where you learn. PoC-to-production is a leap most teams underestimate — take it seriously and take it early.

We are ahead on this at Insly because we started before it was obvious we needed to. That advantage compounds every quarter.

**The insurance companies that will lead in 2028 are the ones building internal AI capability in 2026. Not everyone will do it. That gap is the opportunity.**
